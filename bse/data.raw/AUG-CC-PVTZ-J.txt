The aug-cc-pVTZ-J basis sets developed by Sauer and co-workers are
modifications of the aug-cc-pVTZ basis sets by Dunning and co-workers
(T. H. Dunning, Jr. J. Chem. Phys. 90, 1007 (1989);
 R. A. Kendall, T. H. Dunning, Jr. and R. J. Harrison, J. Chem. Phys. 96, 6769 (1992);
 D. E. Woon and T. H. Dunning, Jr. J. Chem. Phys. J. Chem. Phys. 98, 1358 (1993)).
They were optimized for the calculation of NMR indirect nuclear spin-spin
coupling constants with either correlated wavefunction methods such as SOPPA
and SOPPA(CCSD) or density functional theory methods.
Currently the basis sets exist for:
H, B, C, N, O, F, Al, P, Si, S, Cl, Se 
The composition of the basis sets are:
for H:          (10s3p1d) contracted to [6s3p1d]
for B, C, N, O, F: (15s6p3d1f) contracted to [9s5p3d1f]
for Al, P, Si, S, Cl: (20s10p6d1f) contracted to [10s7p6d1f]
for Se:               (23s14p10d2f) contracted to [12s9p6d2f]
The references for the basis sets are:

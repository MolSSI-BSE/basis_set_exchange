          Core         Name     Primitives       Contractions
Cu - Zn: 10 [Ne]      ECP10MDF  (8s,7p,6d,1f) -> [4s,4p,3d,1f]
Ga - As: 10 [Ne]      ECP10MDF  (8s,7p,7d) ->    [4s,3p,2d]
Ag - Cd: 28 [Ar+3d]   ECP28MDF  (8s,7p,6d,1f) -> [4s,4p,3d,1f]
In - Xe: 28 [Ar+3d]   ECP28MDF  (8s,6p,6d) ->    [4s,3p,2d]
Au - Hg: 60 [Kr+4d3f] ECP60MDF  (8s,7p,6d,1f) -> [4s,4p,3d,1f]
Tl - Rn: 60 [Kr+4d3f] ECP60MDF  (8s,6p,6d) ->    [4s,3p,2d]
Note: although these basis sets are coupled with small core pseudopotentials,
they are only designed to correlate the outermost valence electrons.  For
example, for Cu only the 3d4s electrons are correlated.  This is consistent
with the all-electron transition metal basis sets.  Although there are basis
functions for the 3s3p electrons, they are treated as core.
